Service 01

AI Governance Advisory

Most organizations are using AI in consequential decisions without a documented governance structure, a model inventory, or a defensible audit trail. FCG builds that structure — from policy to board-ready reporting — before the gap becomes a liability.

This engagement is for organizations that are already using AI, evaluating AI deployment, or facing regulatory scrutiny of existing AI use. The output is not a framework document. It is an operating governance model that your team can maintain and your leadership can defend.

What you will have when we are done

AI use case inventory with four-tier risk classification — distinguishing predictive from realized risk

Governance policy aligned to NIST AI RMF and applicable regulatory guidance (SEC, FINRA, or industry-specific)

Model risk escalation protocol with defined thresholds for human review

Board and examiner-ready reporting template with defined cadence

Reversibility classification for each active AI use case

CIOs, CCOs, and CROs at organizations with 50 or more employees that are deploying AI in client-facing, credit, compliance, or operational workflows. Especially relevant for regulated industries — financial services, healthcare, legal — where examiner scrutiny of AI is accelerating.

"We are using AI but have no documented governance structure."
"We could not produce an AI use case inventory for an examiner today."
"Leadership is asking for board-level AI reporting and we do not have a framework for it."
"We know there is regulatory risk in our AI deployment — we just have not addressed it formally."

Advisory engagement. Scoped after a 30-minute discovery session. Delivered over four to eight weeks depending on organization size and complexity.

Service 02

Technology Strategy & Alignment

Technology investment without alignment is experimentation at scale. Organizations consistently report a gap between the technology they fund and the business outcomes they actually achieve. FCG closes that gap by building the current-to-target state framework that connects technology decisions to measurable business results.

This engagement is for organizations where technology spending decisions are made informally, tracked inconsistently, or disconnected from strategic priorities. It is also for organizations undergoing process optimization — identifying where current workflows are creating friction, defining the target state, and building a structured path to get there.

What you will have when we are done

Current-state assessment and gap analysis — where you are, where you need to be, and what is standing between them

Investment prioritization framework tied to business outcomes, not just technical merit

Executive reporting cadence and KPI structure that makes technology performance visible to leadership

Pilot-to-production decision criteria — so experiments become decisions, not permanent pilots

Process optimization recommendations with options and tradeoffs, not just a single prescribed path

CFOs and COOs at growing organizations — from small businesses investing in technology for the first time to mid-market firms with established but uncoordinated technology portfolios. Also relevant to CIOs in organizations where technology strategy and business strategy are not visibly connected.

Service 03

AI Literacy & Adoption (LEAP)

Organizations stop at learning instead of designing for application. Tools are introduced. Training is delivered. Usage is tracked. Impact is not measured. The result is cognitive overload without productivity gains, and AI investments that cannot demonstrate ROI.

LEAP is a structured AI literacy program delivered to leadership and operational teams. It is role-specific, immediately applicable, and designed around one outcome: moving people from understanding AI to using it confidently in real work. Not more training. A bridge from learning to application.

What you will have when we are done

Role-differentiated AI literacy curriculum — executives, managers, and individual contributors each receive what is relevant to their work, not a generic overview

Practical application exercises tied directly to real workflows in your organization

Manager enablement guide for sustaining adoption after the program ends

Pre- and post-literacy assessment to measure actual capability change, not just completion

Defined "what we are removing to make room for this" — the structural conditions that allow adoption to take hold

COOs, CHROs, and Operations leaders at organizations where AI tools have been deployed but adoption is uneven, inconsistent, or creating more cognitive load than it eliminates. Also relevant to leadership teams preparing for an AI deployment and wanting to build readiness before rollout.

"We deployed the tools. People are not using them — or using them inconsistently."
"Training happened. Adoption did not."
"We added AI without removing anything. Now everyone is managing both."
"We cannot measure whether our AI investments are changing how work actually gets done."

Workshop series or embedded advisory. Scoped to team size, existing tool environment, and current adoption baseline. Can be delivered standalone or alongside an AI Governance or Technology Strategy engagement.

A 30-minute discovery session. We will assess your current adoption baseline and identify where the structural gaps are before scoping the program.
